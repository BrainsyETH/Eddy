🦦 What is Eddy? ▾

Eddy is a free, AI-powered float trip planner for Missouri rivers. Named after the swirling currents found along riverbanks, Eddy helps you check real-time water conditions, plan your route, and find access points — all in one place.

Whether you’re a first-time floater or a seasoned river rat, Eddy gives you the information you need to plan a safe and fun trip on the water.

⚙️ How It Works ▾
💧
Live USGS Data
We pull real-time water level and flow data from USGS gauges across Missouri every 15 minutes.
📊
Condition Calculation
Custom thresholds for each gauge determine if conditions are optimal, low, high, or dangerous.
⏱️
Float Time Estimates
We calculate float times based on river distance, current flow rates, and vessel type.
📍
Segment-Aware
Different stretches of the same river can have different conditions — Eddy tracks each segment separately.
🚦 Understanding Condition Codes ▾

Eddy uses a color-coded system to communicate river conditions at a glance:

Optimal Float!
Water levels are ideal for floating. Clear water, good flow, and safe conditions.
Recommended: Great for all skill levels
Okay Decent
Conditions are acceptable but not ideal. May be slightly above or below optimal range.
Recommended: Experienced floaters, shorter trips
Low Wait
Water is below comfortable floating level. Expect shallow spots and potential scraping.
Recommended: Wait for rain or choose a spring-fed river
High Caution
Water is elevated with faster current. Increased hazards including strainers and debris.
Recommended: Experienced paddlers only
Dangerous / Flood Stay Out
Water levels are dangerously high. Flood conditions present extreme risk to life.
DO NOT float under any circumstances
📡 Data Sources ▾
  • USGS National Water Information System — Real-time water level and flow data
  • National Hydrography Dataset (NHD) — River geometry and distance calculations
  • Missouri Department of Conservation — Access point data and regulations
  • National Park Service — Ozark National Scenic Riverways information
  • Recreation.gov — Campground and facility data
⚠️ Safety & Disclaimer ▾
Important Safety Information
  • Eddy is a planning tool only — not a substitute for local knowledge
  • Always check conditions with local outfitters before floating
  • Water levels can change rapidly, especially after rain
  • Always wear a life jacket (PFD)
  • Never float alone
  • Avoid floating in high water or flood conditions
  • Tell someone your float plan before you go
