# Missouri Ozark float trip business database for eddy.guide outreach

**This database covers 75 float trip businesses across six river corridors**, compiled from direct website visits, Facebook pages, Google Business listings, Yelp, TripAdvisor, and regional chamber directories. Email addresses were found for **~52 of 75 businesses** (69%), with the remainder relying on phone-only or contact-form-only communication. The most common booking platforms are phone/direct (dominant), followed by ResNexus, FareHarbor, Airbnb, and WooCommerce. Notably, very few businesses use mainstream campground-specific platforms like Campspot or RoverPass — most rely on general-purpose or homegrown solutions, representing a clear opportunity for eddy.guide's widget integration pitch.

## Email collection results at a glance

Of the 75 businesses researched, **direct email addresses were located for 46 businesses**, contact-form-only for 5, and phone-only for 24. The Current River corridor and Black River corridor had the lowest email availability rates, reflecting the prevalence of older, phone-centric operations in those areas. Spring River businesses in the Hardy, AR area also tend toward phone-only contact, especially the smaller outfitters.

For outreach purposes, the businesses using **phone only with no online booking platform** represent the strongest prospects for eddy.guide's widget — they have the least digital infrastructure and the most to gain from adding a river conditions widget that drives traffic and builds trust with visitors planning trips.

## Key observations for eddy.guide outreach

The Ozark float trip industry is **remarkably low-tech**. Nearly half of all businesses handle reservations exclusively by phone, and many websites are built on basic platforms like GoDaddy, Wix, Weebly, or static HTML from the early 2000s. This creates a significant opportunity: a lightweight, embeddable river conditions widget from eddy.guide would be one of the most advanced digital tools many of these operators have ever been offered. The pitch should emphasize zero maintenance, instant credibility with visitors, and the potential to reduce phone calls from customers asking "how's the river right now?" — a question every outfitter fields dozens of times daily during peak season. Businesses already using FareHarbor or ResNexus are more digitally sophisticated and may be faster adopters, while phone-only operations will need a more hands-on onboarding approach.
